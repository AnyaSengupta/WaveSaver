The Problem

Previous Next

The National Weather Service (NWS) reports that West Coast sneaker waves destroy more people than any other weather hazard. Sneaker waves form during offshore storms that transmit energy to the ocean surface. Meteorologists track oceanic storms to forecast where sneaker waves would reach the Northern California coast days later. In any given year, one or two sneaker wave deaths can occur in the Bay Area. Due to an extraordinarily high number of sneaker waves, a warm spell exacerbated by La Nina weather, and a surge of families using beaches to avoid the pandemic shutdown, the time from November to January was deadlier than usual. The larger issue at hand, many bay area tourists and locals weren’t aware of the risk of sneaker waves, many don’t know what a sneaker wave is and don’t know the dangers of being caught in one.

Solution Idea & Target Consumer

Previous Next

The idea of this project is to create a device to make beach trips safer. Our device takes the form of a telescopic pole that can be quickly used in case of emergencies in high tides and sneaker waves.

Our target consumers are frequent beachgoers, tourists, Bay Area locals, those who live by beaches in Big Sur to the California–Oregon border. Our WaveSaver is built to prevent death from sneaker waves, it’s meant to be used as an emergency tool to survive a sneaker wave attack. The WaveSaver can also be utilized as a signal for help because of its bright colors. Our goal is for this device to become an essential part of an emergency aid kit, and as our device popularizes, further awareness will be spread about the dangers of sneaker waves.
